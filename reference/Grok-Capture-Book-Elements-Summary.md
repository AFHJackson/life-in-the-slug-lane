## Origin Story: The DC Slug Lines

It all started from my experience with the DC slug lines where I thought I was looking at something like a laissez-faire, ultra-capitalist concept where people freely engaged and disengaged in the overall structure of it, and that they were able to define the terms and value of exchanges, and that what they offered was completely up to them, what the other person offered was completely up to them, and that they at will engaged in this value exchange, and the meaning of the exchange was open to anybody's interpretation and their own self-selection for how to pursue and engage with it.

But then one day I had a guy give me a ride after the HOV lanes had closed, and it didn't make any sense. Why was this guy acting where there was no value exchange, where I had nothing to offer, and yet he was acting like a friend or like family?

---

## The Insight: Overlapping Systems

So I've been thinking about that for a while and started to sit back and look and realize that the DC slug lines themselves represent the overlap of so many different human relationship systems:

1. **Capitalist elements:** Prioritizes innovation, freedom of exchange, and private ownership of means of production of value.

2. **Socialist elements:** The underlying system is actually a social system with roadways, infrastructures, police and safety that had set up using commonwealth a set of systems that were available to all and that all were served by equally regardless of the amount they contributed to them.

3. **Anarchical elements:** People could engage and disengage and there were no terms to the engagement, there was no leadership. It was more anarchy in a positive sense than it was really capitalist, which has a marketplace with defined rules and underlying structures and regulations. Very anarchical with people forming friendships and relationships and social structures that were self-adapting and relatively complex.

4. **Communistic elements:** In this guy's offer of a ride where I had a need but nothing to give him, and he offered it anyway—it had a very communistic structure in some really positive sense where it was "from each according to their ability to each according to their need."

---

## The Core Question

Then I started to wonder if these systems couldn't be applied in some cohesive way such that all of them were in play, one of them was prioritized, but they weren't antithetical. They weren't a zero-sum game kind of competition of world ideology or macro ideologies that were simply describing:
- The control of the means of production of value at a macro level for a nation
- Simply choosing metrics for success of that macro ideology like GDP or level of individual freedom

But they were really looking at the root of human phenomena in four different areas that all had interrelated aspects.

**Problem Statement:** The world wasn't lining up with the ideologies.

## The Challenge: The Broken Spectrum

The challenges associated with these are both historical:
- We are presented with these competing ideologies and shown them on a spectrum
- You've got to pick one and defend your camp
- Sometimes quietly accept other constructs but always defending your own construct against them at a macro level

## The Architecture: The Ring

The offering: that line or that range is actually a **ring** and it acts like a compass where depending on where you're operating, the ring rotates. While all four of those ideologies stay in play, one of them is prioritized and the other three are contributing but not driving.

I observe that:
- That interplay is there
- That reality is there
- By actively recognizing this and embracing all the ideologies, they have a strong opportunity for increased value in any given situation

Examples of misapplication:
- A family run like a capitalist structure is nonsensical and horrendous
- A municipality run like a commune is nonsensical
- A friendship run like a social structure or a market...

## Key Dimensions

### Scale
- **Individual to population level** vs. **Individual to individual level**

### Individuality vs. Uniformity
- **Uniform needs:** Common human needs for air, water, and safety
- **Individual needs:** Individualistic priorities associated with value, taste, preference, identity

### Sacrifice and Control
The association of sacrifice with centralization of control:
- In a family it's highly centralized control
- Those who have the most control are the ones who make the greatest sacrifice

## Ideals Within Each Domain

### Capitalism: Obligation to Excess

In Adam Smith's Wealth of Nations, he talks about:
- The underlying basic needs being met
- The ability to specialize
- In specialization, the ability to create surplus

Within the capitalist structure of human relationships, we have an **obligation to excess** where we are driven to excel, specialize, and be able to produce a maximal amount that ultimately spills out as excess.

**Excess defined:** Value created beyond which we can consume and which the marketplace can make use of, such that we can freely give it away.

Excess is what drives and is the source of:
- Innovation and investment
- Where generosity can truly take form

### Communism/Family: Greatest Control = Greatest Sacrifice

Within our families, the ideal is where the greatest control leads to the greatest sacrifice. Those who are in authority are those who give and sacrifice the most.

This principle is rooted in family and in the communist structure of "from each according to their ability to each according to their need."

The family is fed by the basic value generation and surplus.

### Socialism/Municipality: Efficiency in Meeting Common Needs

For our socialism or municipality structure:
- Effectively meeting all needs associated with commonly held human needs
- Leverage the economies of scale associated with joint investments in uniformly held human needs:
  - Air, water, public safety, emergency rescue
  - Roadways, police, fire, emergency rescue, infrastructure
  - Stormwater management, wastewater treatment, clean water

Goals:
- Ensure efficiency to consume as little of the surplus/excess generated in the economy
- Ensure those being left out or failing to benefit from the underlying social structure are included

### Anarchy: Identity Selection and Development

We leverage the anarchy structure for **individual identity selection and development**.

- We may have been born the middle child into an Italian family with all these identity elements placed on us through inheritance
- But in our friendship groups and volunteerism of the anarchy circle, we have the chance to try on identities:
  - "We are the generous one, the brave one, the courageous one, the organized one"
- We get to spend the excess we produce in our capitalist area of specialization in areas of one-on-one investments in each other and in our friendships and communities

## Relationships Among the Four Structures

### Decentralization of Authority
Movement around the ring: Communism → Socialism → Capitalism → Anarchy = Increasing decentralization

### Generosity of Sacrifice
Movement in reverse: Anarchy → Capitalism → Socialism → Communism = Increasing sacrifice (and centralization of control)

### Uniformity vs. Individuality
- **Right side of ring (Socialism + Communism):** Uniformity of giving, structural uniformity
- **Left side of ring (Anarchy + Capitalism):** Individualism

### Preferences vs. Needs
- When working in the area of **preferences**, use tools of capitalism and anarchy
- When working in the area of **needs**, use tools of socialism and communism

### Scale of Interaction
- **Anarchy + Communism (family/friendship):** One-to-one relationships with deep knowledge of the person
- **Capitalism + Socialism (market/municipality):** One-to-many or one-to-population level relationships

**Warning:** Tools that work at one-to-one (depending on high knowledge of the person) should not be expected to work at capitalism or socialism level. When we expect that from those structures, we're fooling ourselves and should expect them to fall apart.
