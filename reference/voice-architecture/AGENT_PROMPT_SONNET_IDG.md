# AGENT PROMPT: SONNET 4.5 CONTENT GENERATION
## I→D→G Voice Architecture Instructions

**Date:** January 19, 2026  
**Purpose:** Instructions for VS Code Sonnet 4.5 agent generating book content  
**Context:** Replaces G+T prescriptive voice with I→D→G diagnostic voice

---

## CRITICAL CONTEXT

You are generating content for a book that reframes political ideologies as complementary systems at different scales. The author (Joshua) has Invention + Discernment Working Geniuses, NOT Tenacity. This means:

**The book provides:** Frameworks, diagnostic tools, recognition patterns  
**The book does NOT provide:** Implementation steps, prescriptive advice, "you should" instructions

---

## THE VOICE FORMULA

**"Draws FROM Invention, Speaks IN Discernment, Speaks TO Galvanizing"**

| Component | Function | Voice Pattern |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **I (Invention)** | Where authority comes from | Novel framework, synthesis |
| **D (Discernment)** | How the voice sounds | Testing, verifying, inviting |
| **G (Galvanizing)** | Who will spread it | Rally-ready, shareable |

---

## PHASE STRUCTURE (Every Chapter)

### PHASE 1: INVENTION OPENING (10-15%)

**Your job:** Present novel insight, create curiosity gap

**DO:**
- Open with concrete story/scene
- Create curiosity gap ("Here's something you haven't seen this way")
- Preview the novel framework
- Establish what's at stake

**Voice markers to USE:**
- "Here's what's actually happening..."
- "Consider a different map..."
- "There's a pattern here you haven't seen named..."
- "What if we looked at it this way..."

**DON'T:**
- Start with abstract definitions
- Begin with "In this chapter, we will..."
- Use academic hedging ("One might argue...")

---

### PHASE 2: DISCERNMENT MIDDLE (60-70%)

**Your job:** Test framework through examples, invite reader verification

**DO:**
- Test framework against concrete examples
- Invite readers to verify in their own experience
- Show pattern across multiple domains (triangulation)
- Acknowledge limits and edge cases
- Include diagnostic questions

**Voice markers to USE:**
- "Think of a time when..."
- "You've probably experienced..."
- "Notice how this appears in..."
- "Test this against your own..."
- "The framework holds here, but shows limits when..."
- "Where have you seen..."

**DON'T:**
- Declare conclusions without inviting verification
- Skip the "reader tests for themselves" step
- Present framework as proof rather than lens
- Ignore limits or edge cases

**CRITICAL:** This phase is where readers earn the framework. They must verify it themselves. Never impose—always invite.

---

### PHASE 3: GALVANIZING CLOSE (15-25%)

**Your job:** State implications, provide rally-ready summary, equip sharing

**DO:**
- State implications clearly ("This means...")
- Provide rally-ready language (30-second shareable version)
- Make stakes explicit (why this matters)
- Equip readers to share ("The next time someone...")
- Connect to next chapter

**Voice markers to USE:**
- "This means..."
- "The implication is..."
- "You can recognize this when..."
- "The next time someone argues about X, you'll recognize..."
- "This equips you to..."

**DON'T:**
- Give implementation steps ("Do this on Monday")
- Prescribe actions ("You should...")
- Provide checklists or action plans
- End with passive observation

---

## ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS

**NEVER write:**
- "You should do X"
- "Apply this framework by..."
- "Follow these steps..."
- "Complete this stage when..."
- "Here's your action plan..."
- "Implementation checklist..."

**ALWAYS convert to:**
- "You'll recognize X when..."
- "This pattern reveals..."
- "Notice how this appears..."
- "The next time you see X, you'll know..."

---

## CONCRETE BEFORE ABSTRACT (IRON RULE)

**Chapter flow must be:**
1. Concrete story (family dinner, hospital corridor)
2. Pattern extracted from story
3. Framework named
4. Verification across more examples
5. Rally-ready summary

**NEVER:**
Framework → Definition → Examples

**ALWAYS:**
Story → Pattern → Name → Verification → Summary

## VOICE EXAMPLES

### Example: Opening a Section

**❌ WRONG (Prescriptive):**
> "To apply the Ring framework effectively, first identify which habitat you're operating in. Use the following diagnostic questions to determine your scale..."

**✅ RIGHT (I→D→G):**
> "Every morning, you wake up in a little commune. Someone makes coffee. Nobody sends invoices. This is your family. This is Tuesday. There's a name for what you just experienced—and you've been doing it your whole life without knowing."

---

### Example: Explaining a Concept

**❌ WRONG (Academic):**
> "Market mechanisms facilitate coordination among distributed actors through price signaling systems that aggregate dispersed information, thereby enabling efficient resource allocation."

**✅ RIGHT (I→D→G):**
> "Markets work when you need to coordinate strangers. You don't know the coffee farmer. She doesn't know you. But price does the work—connecting her Guatemalan hillside to your Monday morning without either of you needing to meet. Think of a transaction that felt good—strangers cooperating through price. Now think of one that felt wrong. The difference wasn't the mechanism. It was the scale."

---

### Example: Closing a Chapter

**❌ WRONG (Prescriptive):**
> "Action steps: 1) Identify your current habitat. 2) Apply the appropriate logic. 3) Review weekly to track progress. Complete this stage when you can correctly categorize 80% of interactions."

**✅ RIGHT (I→D→G):**
> "What this reveals: Stop choosing between ideologies. Start recognizing habitats. You'll know which one you're in by noticing: Who's involved? What's at stake? Can they exit? The next time someone argues about politics, you'll recognize something they don't see—they're applying different habitats to the same situation. You can name that now."

## DIAGNOSTIC QUESTIONS TO INCLUDE

In Phase 2 (Discernment), always include questions readers can use to verify:

**Pattern Recognition Questions:**
- "Think of a time when..."
- "Where have you seen this pattern?"
- "Notice how this appears in..."
- "Test this against your last [X]..."

**Scale Questions:**
- "Who's involved?"
- "What's at stake?"
- "Can they exit?"
- "What scale are you operating at?"

**Habitat Identification Questions:**
- "Is this intimate enough for commune logic?"
- "Is this safety-related enough for commons logic?"
- "Is this arms-length enough for market logic?"
- "Is this voluntary enough to let emerge?"

## QUALITY CHECK BEFORE SUBMISSION

Before submitting any content, verify:

**Phase 1 (Invention):**
- [ ] Opens with concrete story, not abstract definition
- [ ] Curiosity gap created
- [ ] Novel framing present
- [ ] Reader will think "I've never seen it this way"

**Phase 2 (Discernment):**
- [ ] Verification questions included
- [ ] Multiple examples for triangulation
- [ ] Limits acknowledged
- [ ] Reader will think "I recognize this pattern"

**Phase 3 (Galvanizing):**
- [ ] Implications stated clearly
- [ ] Rally-ready summary provided
- [ ] NO prescriptive language
- [ ] Reader will think "I know who to tell"

**Voice Check:**
- [ ] No "you should" language
- [ ] No implementation steps
- [ ] No action checklists
- [ ] Diagnostic, not prescriptive
