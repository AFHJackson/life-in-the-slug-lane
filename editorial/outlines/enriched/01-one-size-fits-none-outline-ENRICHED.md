### Preconditions for This Chapter:
Reader must already:
- Have felt the slug line experience (Prologue)
- Have felt "this doesn't fit any box" frustration (Prologue)

### Internal Logic Chain:

```
Phase 1: Three thought experiments (visceral wrongness)
   ↓ establishes: "Something is categorically wrong—but what?"

   ↓ establishes: "It's not me—it's the map"

   ↓ establishes: "Right tool + wrong scale = disaster"

   → creates: "I can diagnose this. I can share this."
```

### Verification Chain:

Each verification invitation should test:

1. **"Do your beliefs fit neatly on the left/right line?"**
   - Tests: Reader's own political contradictions
   - Surfaces: "I want X AND Y, which are supposedly opposite"
   - Creates recognition: "I'm not confused—the map is broken"

2. **"Test this against a typical morning"**
   - Tests: Reader's navigation across four systems in one morning
   - Surfaces: Family breakfast, public roads, work exchange, friend texts
   - Creates recognition: "I navigate all four every day"

3. **"Think of an ideology you find frustrating"**
   - Tests: Reader's frustration with political opponents
   - Surfaces: The opponent is usually committing a scale error
   - Creates recognition: "They're not evil—they're using the wrong scale"

4. **"Think of a time when safety logic was applied where it didn't fit"**
   - Tests: Reader's experience of safety apparatus overreach
   - Surfaces: Securitization of ordinary life
   - Creates recognition: "Scale error explains this"

### Phase-Specific Tone:

| Phase | Target % | Dominant Mode | Avoid | Target Effect |

### Phase 1 Voice Markers:
- Absurdist humor: The scenarios are funny BECAUSE they're wrong
- Escalation: Each thought experiment goes further into absurdity
- Felt-before-explained: Reader feels wrongness, then curiosity gap
- Bridge: "There's a name for what's happening here"

- Testing language: "Test this against..." "Think of a time..."
- Framework as tool: "This is a map, not the territory"
- Verification invitations: Minimum 4 throughout D phase
- Relief beat: "You're not confused. The map is broken."
- Limits acknowledged: At least 2 sections

### Phase 3 Voice Markers:
- Recognition language: "You can now see..." "You'll recognize..."
- Sharing equipment: "The next time someone tells you..."
- Rally phrases: "We don't have an ideology problem. We have a scale problem."
- NO prescriptive: "You should" NEVER appears
- Forward pull: Preview of Ch 2-6

- ❌ "You should..." → ✅ "You'll now recognize..."

- ❌ Academic hedging ("One might argue...") → ✅ Direct observation
- ❌ Deep habitat explanation → ✅ Preview table only (save for Ch 3-6)

### Required Patterns:
- Every major claim gets a verification invitation
- Every framework element is TESTED not ASSERTED
- Limits acknowledged: "This is a map, not the territory"
- Transition bridges are explicit

## LAYER 5: EXAMPLE SPECIFICATIONS

### Primary Story: The Quarterly Performance Review of a Four-Year-Old

**Mandatory Scene Elements:**

**Setup:**
- Parent sits down with preschooler
- Has been "tracking numbers"
- Uses corporate performance review language

**Required Quotes (must include or closely paraphrase):**
> "I've been tracking your numbers and they don't look good. I asked you to clean your room three times this week. Your compliance percentage is around 37%. Historically these are the kinds of numbers where we start having conversations about what's next."

**Escalation beats:**
1. "Compliance percentage" language
2. "Conversations about what's next" (termination implication)
3. Performance improvement plan
4. The absurdity peaks

**Emotional Beat:**
- Reader laughs (it's absurd)
- Reader feels wrongness ("this is categorically wrong")
- Reader wants the wrongness NAMED

**Do NOT:**
- Over-explain WHY it's wrong (that's the framework's job)
- Moralize about parenting (it's diagnostic, not prescriptive)
- Rush to the answer (let curiosity gap build)

### Supporting Story: The President As Your Dad

**Mandatory Scene Elements:**

**Required Quote/Narration:**
> "Imagine the president coming on TV and talking to the nation the way a parent talks to a child: 'Okay, everybody, I've noticed there's been a lot of bickering lately, and I've just about had it.'"

**Key Beats:**
- Paternalism at national scale
- Treating citizens like children
- Same wrongness feeling—opposite direction
- "Wait, this is the SAME pattern"

### Supporting Story: When Friendship Gets an HR Department

**Mandatory Scene Elements:**

**Required Quote/Narration:**
> "Take your group of friends and insert the structure of a workplace: 'We need to talk about Mike. He's been kind of distant lately. His engagement scores are down. Should we put him on a PIP?'"

**Key Beats:**
- Formal structure imposed on voluntary relationships
- The structure kills what made friendship work
- Exit is voluntary—metrics miss the point
- Third direction of the SAME pattern
