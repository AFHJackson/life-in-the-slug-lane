## LAYER 5: EXAMPLE SPECIFICATIONS

### Primary Story: Family Dinner Scene (Jada and Kenji)

**Mandatory Scene Elements:**

**Characters (all must appear by name):**
- Jada (mom, serves and eats last)
- Kenji (dad, pours milk, gets up twice)
- Grandma Diane (Kenji's mom, cooked all afternoon, holds baby)
- Marcus, 9 (complains about broccoli)
- Kai, 6 (sauce on forehead, needs help cutting)
- Naomi, 4 months (staring at ceiling fan, in bouncer)

**Setting:**
- Tuesday night dinner table
- Family home
- Multi-generational
- Ordinary chaos

**Key Moments (all must appear):**

1. **Uneven Giving (must show):**
   - Diane cooked all afternoon, eats almost nothing, seems satisfied
   - Kenji loads kids' plates first, finishes what they don't
   - Jada takes what's left, ensures Diane has extra vegetables

2. **Chaos Without Score (must show):**
   - Marcus knocks over milk
   - Kai needs help cutting food
   - Naomi starts fussing, Diane scoops her up without being asked
   - Kenji gets up twice (napkins, water, different fork)

3. **The Absence of Transaction (must convey):**
   - "Nobody was tracking any of this. Nobody was counting."
   - "The accounting books were gloriously uneven"
   - "Nobody experienced this as unfair"

4. **The Revelation (must state):**
   - "From each according to their ability, to each according to their need"
   - "They didn't know they were quoting anyone"
   - "They called it family"

**Emotional Beat:**
- Reader should feel: "That's my kitchen" (or kitchens they know)
- Reader should feel warmth before analysis
- Reader should be surprised by "communism" label

**Do NOT:**
- Make the family perfect (it's chaotic)
- Lecture about what they're doing (let scene show)
- Skip the generational layers (grandma is essential)

### Historical Examples: One Big Family Fallacy

**Must be brief and vivid (not lectures):**

1. **Soviet collectivization:**
   - From-each-to-each by FORCE
   - Without intimacy, obligation becomes coercion
   - "What happens without intimacy"

2. **Mao's communes:**
   - Family logic without family bonds
   - "What happens without trust"

3. **Any dynasty treating nation as property:**
   - Family logic at population scale
   - "What's love at family scale is corruption at nation scale"
