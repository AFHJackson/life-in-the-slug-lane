## HEAD-TO-HEAD: KEY PASSAGES

### Opening Line

**Opus:**
> "Nobody organized this. Nobody runs it. And it works every single day."

**Sonnet:**
> "The cold finds you first."

**Winner:** Sonnet. Both work, but Sonnet's is more visceral and immediate. Opus's is more thematic but slightly tells-before-shows.

---

### Green Acura Driver Description

**Opus:**
> "That's when a little green Acura pulled up, rolled down the window, and offered us a ride."

**Sonnet:**
> "The window came down. The driver was maybe mid-forties, coffee in the cup holder, NPR voices murmuring from the radio. She looked at us with something between amusement and sympathy."

**Winner:** Sonnet. The sensory details (coffee, NPR, the look on her face) make the scene live.

---

### The Insight Statement

**Opus:**
> "Maybe the problem isn't that we picked the wrong team on that ideological line. Maybe the problem is the line itself."

**Sonnet:**
> "The problem wasn't that I'd picked the wrong team. The problem was treating them like teams at all."

**Winner:** Sonnet. Tighter, more quotable, slightly different emphasis (systems as tools, not teams).

---

### Closing Line

**Opus:**
> "There's a pattern here. Let me show you what I found."

**Sonnet:**
> "There's a pattern here. And once you see it, you can't unsee it."

**Winner:** Sonnet. "You can't unsee it" creates more anticipation than "let me show you."
